# Common CV Mistakes and Fixes

## Buzzwords to Eliminate

Remove entirely or replace with evidence:

- Hardworking, team player, go-getter, self-starter, passionate
- Detail-oriented, results-driven, innovative, motivated, dynamic
- Proactive, people person, out-of-the-box thinker
- "Proven track record" -- prove it with numbers instead
- "Synergy", "leverage", "paradigm", "utilize" -- pretentious jargon

## Outdated Phrases to Remove

- "Objective statement" -- replace with Professional Summary
- "References available upon request" -- assumed; wastes space
- "Microsoft Office proficient" -- assumed baseline
- "Proficient in email" -- do not include

## Weak Verb Replacements

| Weak | Strong Alternatives |
|------|-------------------|
| Responsible for | Managed, Led, Owned, Directed |
| Helped with | Collaborated on, Contributed to (with specifics) |
| Worked on | Engineered, Developed, Architected |
| Handled | Orchestrated, Streamlined, Executed |
| Participated in | Drove, Co-led, Spearheaded |
| Was involved in | Delivered, Implemented, Championed |

## Structural Mistakes

1. **Listing duties instead of achievements**
   - Bad: "Managed a team of 5"
   - Good: "Led team of 5 engineers to deliver payment gateway integration 2 weeks ahead of schedule, processing $3.2M in first-month transactions"

2. **No quantification** -- at least 60% of bullets must have numbers

3. **Generic, one-size-fits-all resume** -- tailoring per application is mandatory

4. **Using personal pronouns** -- never write "I developed..."; start with the verb "Developed..."

5. **Including a photo** (in US/UK/Canada applications)

6. **Inconsistent formatting** -- dates, fonts, spacing must be uniform

7. **Irrelevant hobbies** -- "Enjoy hiking and cooking" adds nothing unless directly relevant

8. **Typos** -- one typo can eliminate a candidate. Proofread with tools AND a human.

9. **Padding with technologies used once** -- only list skills defendable in an interview

10. **Skills progress bars/ratings** -- "Python: 90%" is meaningless and ATS-invisible

## Cover Letter Alignment

- Use same font family, sizes, header style, and accent color as the CV
- Do NOT restate resume bullets -- add the story behind 1-2 key achievements
- Address the company's specific needs; reference the job posting
- Keep to one page (3-4 paragraphs)
- Structure: Opening hook -> Why this company -> Strongest achievement with context -> Call to action
- Write in natural voice -- generic AI-generated content is increasingly detected and penalized
